I devised a curl command  line for local nodes that shows what pNodes are publicly available ending with a total count.
Please replace "127.0.0.1" to a publicly available pNode IP address when you're not running a node yourself (but then again you have to somehow acquire one üòâ): 
curl -X POST http://127.0.0.1:6000/rpc \
                          -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
                          -d '{
                            "jsonrpc": "2.0",
                            "method": "get-pods-with-stats",
                            "id": 1
                          }' |
                     jq . |                                   
                     grep -B1 '"is_public": true' |
                     grep '"address"' |
                     tee /dev/tty |
                     wc -l |
                     awk '{print "Total public nodes:", $1}'
 
Port numbers are added, because a few are on nonstandard ports (not on port 9001).
So devs can pick one to call.
